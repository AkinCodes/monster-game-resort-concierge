What Actually Happens to the Database?

The single most important thing to understand about MemoryStore

⚠ The database is NOT wiped clean. Only the 12 summarised messages are deleted. Everything else stays.

🍪 Sesame Street Version:

Elmo has a jar with 12 marbles in it. The Count squishes them into one crystal.

Does the JAR get thrown away? NO!

Does the SHELF get thrown away? NO!

Does Grover's NOTEBOOK get thrown away? NO!

Only the 12 marbles disappear. The jar stays. The shelf stays. The crystal goes into Grover's notebook. And the jar starts filling up again with new marbles.

Here's what the database looks like at each stage:

BEFORE summarisation (12 messages in the jar):

sessions table:  session_id=abc, summary=NULL

messages table:  msg 1, msg 2, msg 3 ... msg 12

AFTER summarisation (The Count squishes!):

sessions table:  session_id=abc, summary="Guest asked about Vampire Manor and booked a Coffin Suite"

messages table:  (empty — all 12 deleted)

Guest sends message 13 (jar starts filling again):

sessions table:  session_id=abc, summary="Guest asked about Vampire Manor and booked a Coffin Suite"

messages table:  msg 13

Messages 14–24 arrive (jar is full again!):

sessions table:  same summary

messages table:  msg 13, msg 14 ... msg 24 (12 again!)

Summarisation triggers AGAIN:

sessions table:  summary="Guest asked about Vampire Manor and booked a Coffin Suite
                     \nGuest then asked about spa treatments and check-out times"

messages table:  (empty again)

💡 The key insight:

The summary grows (new summary appended to old summary).

The messages cycle (fill up to 12, get compressed, start over).

The session is never deleted. The summary is never deleted. Only the messages get pruned after being compressed into the summary.

The Six Rituals

Every method in MemoryStore — click to inspect its secrets

🚪
ensure_session()
The Doorkeeper
Opens the guest book. If the visitor is new, writes their name. If returning, updates the timestamp.
📜
add_message()
The Scribe
Drops a marble in the jar. Blue for guests, amber for the concierge. Checks if it's time to summarise.
📖
get_messages()
The Archivist
Opens the jar and peeks inside. Returns up to 50 marbles in order, oldest first. Doesn't remove any.
🔮
get_summary()
The Chronicler
Reads from the tiny notebook. One sentence that captures everything discussed so far. Or nothing, if no summary exists.
⚡
_maybe_summarise()
The Alchemist
Counts the marbles. At 12: lightning strikes! The Wise Owl or the Grouch compresses 12 marbles into one crystal. Old marbles vanish.
🔎
_cheap_summary()
The Scavenger
Rummages through the trash. Shouts out any bookings, names, or invoices. Not smart, but fast and free.
🏪
Think of It as a Hotel Room
One analogy that connects all six rituals together
Your session ID is your room number. That's it. That's the whole idea.

Everything that happens inside MemoryStore is stuff you do inside that room. The six methods above? They're the six things that can happen between you and that room. Let's walk through each one.
🔢
ensure_session()
You swipe your key card at the door. Never been here before? The hotel creates a fresh room for you — new bed, clean notepad, your name on the door. Stayed here last night? The door just opens. The front desk writes down "came back today" and that's it. No new room. Same room. Same stuff inside.
📝
add_message()
You're inside the room now. You say something, and it gets written on the notepad by the bed. The concierge replies — that gets written too. Every single thing either of you says, in order, one line after another. The notepad just keeps growing.
📖
get_messages()
You pick up the notepad and flip through it. Everything is there, from the first thing you said to the most recent. You're just reading — you're not tearing pages out or adding anything. You put it back down exactly as you found it.
📋
get_summary()
There's a small card on the desk — like the hotel welcome card, but this one says what you've talked about so far. "Guest asked about booking a room for Dracula, spa treatments, and check-in times." One card. The short version of everything.
⚡
_maybe_summarise()
Housekeeping. Every time you write something on the notepad, the housekeeper peeks in and counts the pages. Eleven pages? "Not yet." But the moment it hits twelve — housekeeping kicks in. They read all twelve pages, write a short summary on the desk card, and rip out the old pages. The room is tidy again. The summary card remembers what was said. The notepad is fresh for new conversations.
🔎
_cheap_summary()
Sometimes the professional housekeeper is unavailable (the LLM is down or not configured). So the hotel sends in an intern instead. The intern can't write beautiful summaries — they just scan the old pages and shout out anything that looks important: names, booking numbers, dates. It's messy, but it's fast, it's free, and it captures the important bits. Better than nothing.
A Day in the Life of Room 42
9:00 AM — Check In
Count Dracula swipes his key card. ensure_session("room-42") fires. The hotel has never seen this guest before, so a new room is created. Fresh notepad. Empty desk card. His name goes on the door.
9:01 AM — First Words
Dracula: "What blood types are on the spa menu?" add_message("room-42", "user", ...) writes it on the notepad. The concierge replies with the spa menu. That gets written too. Two lines on the notepad.
9:05 AM — 10:30 AM — Long Chat
Dracula keeps asking questions. Bookings, amenities, nearby restaurants, coffin thread counts. Each message gets written on the notepad. The notepad fills up: 4... 7... 10... 11 pages.
10:31 AM — Page 12 — Housekeeping!
_maybe_summarise("room-42") kicks in. The housekeeper reads all 12 pages, writes a summary on the desk card: "Guest asked about spa, bookings, restaurants, and coffin options." Then she rips out the 12 old pages. The notepad is clean again. The desk card remembers everything.
10:32 AM — The Concierge Prepares
Next time the concierge needs to respond, she reads the desk card first (get_summary), then reads whatever's on the notepad (get_messages). She knows the full story — even the parts from the torn-out pages — because the summary captured them.
11:00 AM — 6:00 PM — More Chatting
The cycle repeats. Notepad fills up again. At 12 pages, housekeeping runs again. The desk card gets another line added. Old pages ripped out. This can happen forever — the room never runs out of notepad space because housekeeping keeps tidying up.
11:00 PM — Dracula Leaves
Dracula goes to bed. The room sits empty. Nothing happens to it. The notepad stays on the bed. The desk card stays on the desk. The room number is still on the door. Everything waits.
Next Day, 9:00 AM — Dracula Returns
He swipes his key card again. ensure_session("room-42") fires. The hotel recognizes him — same room, same stuff. Just updates the timestamp: "came back today." The notepad, the desk card, everything is exactly where he left it. He picks up right where he stopped.
The Key Insight

Other guests can't see your notepad. Guest room-42 has their room. Guest room-789 has theirs. The walls between rooms are the session_id — it's the first argument to almost every function because it tells MemoryStore which room to walk into.

The room doesn't care if you leave for 5 minutes or 5 days. When you come back with that same room number, everything is still on the notepad where you left it.

And the room itself is never deleted. The summary card is never deleted. Only the notepad pages get ripped out after being compressed into the summary. The room survives forever.

"The session ID is your room number. The notepad is your messages. The desk card is your summary. Housekeeping keeps it all manageable. And the room never forgets you."
